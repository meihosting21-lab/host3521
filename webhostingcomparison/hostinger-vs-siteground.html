Infrastructure & Architecture

The biggest difference lies in the "bare-metal" foundation. SiteGround has migrated completely to the Google Cloud Platform (GCP). This guarantees enterprise-level redundancy; if a server fails, your site instantly mirrors to another node. It is the same tech powering YouTube.

Hostinger uses its own global data centers running on LiteSpeed Enterprise servers. While not "Cloud" in the same sense as GCP, LiteSpeed is aggressively optimized for PHP execution, often outperforming Apache in raw throughput tests.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which host offers better performance?
It depends on your definition of performance. Hostinger utilizes LiteSpeed servers, which are incredible for raw speed and handling WordPress caching efficiently. SiteGround, however, runs on the premium Google Cloud Platform, offering unmatched stability, redundancy, and consistent uptime, especially for business-critical sites.
Why is SiteGround more expensive?
SiteGround positions itself as a "Premium Managed" host. You are paying for their world-class "Instant Reply" support team, daily backups, and advanced developer tools (Git, Staging). If you are running an e-commerce store or a client's business, this extra cost provides peace of mind. Hostinger, on the other hand, focuses on automation to keep costs incredibly low for personal projects and blogs.
Which is better for beginners?
Both are excellent, but they have different approaches. Hostinger's hPanel is visually stunning, icon-based, and feels like a modern smartphone app—perfect for absolute beginners. SiteGround's Site Tools is also user-friendly but offers more depth and control, which is great if you plan to learn more about website management as you grow.
